Coach's Corner

When Bob proudly said "fuzzy wabbit," you didn't correct him — you just said it back the right way: "That is a super soft, hopping rabbit!" This is called gentle repeating, and it helps Bob hear the right sound without feeling embarrassed, so he stayed relaxed and kept talking instead of going quiet. You also didn't quiz him on saying it "correctly," which kept things feeling like play, not a test — probably why he stayed focused for a full 5 minutes today instead of his usual 2.

Because you didn't correct him, Bob is learning that talking to you feels safe, not scary — and that's likely why he's picking up new words so fast. Tomorrow, try the same trick with two or three more animal sounds: just repeat what he says back the right way, and he'll keep learning without ever feeling like he got something wrong.
